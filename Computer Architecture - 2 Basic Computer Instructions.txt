6) <<<<<<<<<<<<<<<<< TIMING DIAGRAM of MOV INSTRUCTION >>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>

+ Timing Diagram of MOV Instruction in Microprocessor

+ Problem – Draw the timing diagram of the given instruction in 8085,
+ Example: 
+ Algorithm -

+ In Opcode fetch ( t1-t4 T states ):

A) 00 – lower bit of address where opcode is stored, i.e., 00
B) 20 – higher bit of address where opcode is stored, i.e., 20.
C) ALE – provides signal for multiplexed address and data bus. Only in t1 it used as D) address bus to fetch lower bit of address otherwise it will be used as data bus.
E) RD (low active) – signal is 1 in t1 & t4 as no data is read by microprocessor. F) Signal is 0 in t2 & t3 because here the data is read by microprocessor.
G) WR (low active) – signal is 1 throughout, no data is written by microprocessor.
H) IO/M (low active) – signal is 1 in throughout because the operation is performing on memory.
I) S0 and S1 – both are 1 in case of opcode fetching.
